# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Project teams, hospitality operators, luxury retailers, residential developers, and brand decision-makers evaluating fragrance strategy for Saudi Arabia and the GCC. They are usually balancing experience goals with operational realities, and they need a site that feels immediately credible, premium, and easy to act on.

## Product Purpose
Air Aroma's public site should position the brand as a high-trust scent strategy partner for luxury spaces. It needs to communicate fragrance expertise, diffuser capability, and project readiness with clarity, while moving visitors toward consultation, product exploration, and deeper brand confidence.

## Brand Personality
Architectural, sensory, assured.

The site should feel spatial, composed, and materially rich, with the calm confidence of a luxury hospitality environment rather than the flash of a trend-led marketing page. Copy and interaction should feel deliberate, polished, and expert without becoming cold or generic.

## Anti-references
- Generic dark-and-gold luxury templates that rely on color alone for perceived quality
- Glassmorphism used as a default decorative layer
- Reflex editorial-luxury styling that leans on expected serif tropes instead of spatial design
- Repeated SaaS-style card grids, metric strips, and identical section scaffolding
- Any UI that feels AI-generated, overly ornamental, or interchangeable with a premium landing-page template

## Design Principles
- Lead with atmosphere, then prove capability: the first impression should feel premium before the site starts explaining products.
- Use space as the primary luxury signal: hierarchy, pacing, and composition should do more than borders, gradients, or effects.
- Keep conversion calm and confident: CTAs should be present and persuasive without breaking the tone.
- Make bilingual quality non-negotiable: English and Arabic should both feel designed, not merely translated.
- Connect beauty to operational trust: every visual decision should support the feeling that Air Aroma can deliver reliably in real spaces.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA across contrast, focus visibility, keyboard access, touch targets, and readable typography. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`, ensure Arabic layouts remain comfortable and legible, and avoid interaction patterns that depend on hover alone.
